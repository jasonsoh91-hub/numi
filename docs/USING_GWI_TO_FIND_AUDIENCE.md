## Step 2: Explore Audience Size & Demographics

### In GWI, run these reports:

#### Report 1: Audience Overview
```
What it tells you:
- Total reachable audience size
- Age breakdown
- Gender split
- Income distribution
- Education levels
- Professional breakdown

Look for:
- Is this audience large enough? (aim for 5M+ in US)
- Any surprising demographic patterns?
- Are they concentrated in specific cities?
```

#### Report 2: Psychographic Profile
```
What it tells you:
- Personality traits
- Values system
- Life priorities
- Media attitudes
- Purchase drivers

Look for:
- What motivates them?
- What do they fear/worry about?
- What problems are they trying to solve?
- How do they make decisions?
```

#### Report 3: Media Consumption
```
What it tells you:
- Social platforms used
- Content consumed
- Devices used
- Time spent online
- Ad receptiveness

Look for:
- Which platforms dominate? (Meta, TikTok, YouTube, etc.)
- What type of content engages them?
- Mobile vs desktop split
- Best times to reach them
```

#### Report 4: Purchase Behavior
```
What it tells you:
- Subscription services used
- Online purchase frequency
- Price sensitivity
- Category spending
- Brands they love

Look for:
- Do they pay for subscriptions? (which ones?)
- Average transaction value
- Luxury vs mainstream preferences
- Category affinities (wellness, education, tech)
```

## Step 3: Cross-Analysis for Deeper Insights

### Analysis 1: Find the Overlap

**Create separate audiences and compare:**

| Audience | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| **A** | Astrology interest |
| **B** | Personal Development interest |
| **C** | Wellness app users |
| **D** | High income professionals |

**Run audience overlap reports** to find:
- A ∩ B (Astrology + Personal Dev)
- A ∩ B ∩ C (+ Wellness apps)
- A ∩ B ∩ C ∩ D (+ High income)

**This reveals your sweet spot audience size**

---

### Analysis 2: Compare Your Audience to General Population

**Run comparison report:**
```
Your Audience vs Gen Pop

Look for:
- Index scores (100 = average, 150 = 50% more likely)
- What makes them unique?
- What's "normal" for them that's rare for others?
```

**Use these insights for:**
- Messaging that resonates
- Creative direction
- Partnership opportunities
- PR angles
